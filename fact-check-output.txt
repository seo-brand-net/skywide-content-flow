What Is a PDH?
PDH stands for Professional Development Hour--the standard unit for measuring continuing edu-
cation activities for licensed professional engineers. One PDH represents one contact hour of in-
struction, presentation, or participation in qualifying professional development activities. Wiscon-
sin's PDH system follows the framework established by the National Council of Examiners for En-
gineering and Surveying (NCEES), ensuring consistency with national standards while maintaining
state-specific requirements.

Wisconsin PE Continuing Education Requirements at a Glance
Wisconsin PE continuing education requirements mandate 30 PDH hours during each two-year
renewal cycle. Within those 30 hours, at least 2 must focus on professional conduct or ethics--a
requirement that strengthens ethical standards across the profession. The Wisconsin Department of
Safety and Professional Services (DSPS) oversees these requirements, conducting random audits
to ensure compliance.
What makes Wisconsin's approach particularly engineer-friendly is the flexibility built into the sys-
tem. Unlike some states that restrict online learning or cap certain activities, Wisconsin allows en-
gineers to earn PDH credits for engineers through various methods. You can complete all 30 hours
online if that fits your schedule, mix in-person conferences with self-study programs, or combine
teaching with traditional coursework. This flexibility recognizes that professional engineers have
diverse learning styles and scheduling constraints.
What Activities Count Toward Wisconsin PE PDH Credit?
Wisconsin recognizes an extensive range of professional development activities for PDH credit.
Formal coursework from ABET-accredited institutions earns hour-for-hour credit, making university
courses an excellent option for in-depth learning. Professional society meetings qualify when they
include substantive technical content--not just networking or business discussions. The actual pre-
sentation time counts, so a two-hour technical session at an ASCE meeting earns 2 PDHs.
Publishing technical papers in peer-reviewed journals can earn up to 10 PDHs per publication, re-
warding engineers who contribute to the profession's knowledge base. Patent applications also earn
10 PDHs each, recognizing the innovation and technical expertise required. Teaching engineering
courses for the first time earns triple credit (3 PDHs per classroom hour), while repeat teaching
earns single credit up to 15 PDHs per renewal period.
Self-study programs qualify if they include a proficiency test demonstrating mastery of the mater-
ial. Employer-sponsored training counts when it's technical in nature and goes beyond normal job

CEU to PDH Conversion: How the Math Works
Understanding CEU to PDH conversion is essential for maximizing your continuing education op-
tions. The conversion follows the International Association for Continuing Education and Training
(IACET) standard: 1 CEU equals 10 PDH hours. This 10:1 ratio makes calculations simple. A course
offering 0.3 CEUs provides 3 PDHs. A workshop worth 0.5 CEUs delivers 5 PDHs. Partial CEUs
convert proportionally--a 0.15 CEU course equals 1.5 PDHs.
This standardized conversion applies universally in Wisconsin, whether courses come from univer-
sities, professional associations, or specialized online providers. Many course providers list both
CEU and PDH values, but knowing the conversion helps you evaluate options from providers who
may only list one or the other. Remember that some providers use different terminology--contact
hours, credit hours, or learning units--so always verify the PDH equivalent before enrolling.
What Does NOT Count as PDH Credit in Wisconsin?
Understanding what doesn't qualify for PDH credit is just as important as knowing what does. Regu-
lar work duties, no matter how technical or challenging, don't count. Business meetings, even those
discussing engineering projects, don't qualify unless they include documented technical presen-
tations. General management training, leadership development, and personal growth courses fall
outside PDH eligibility unless they contain substantial engineering-specific content.
First aid, CPR, and general safety training typically don't qualify, though safety courses with signif-
icant engineering content might. Facility tours earn no credit unless accompanied by documented
technical presentations lasting at least 30 minutes. Simply maintaining membership in professional
organizations doesn't count--you need active participation in technical activities. Sales presenta-
tions, vendor demonstrations, and marketing seminars don't qualify, even when they involve engi-
neering products or services.

Documentation: What to Keep and for How Long
Wisconsin requires maintaining PDH documentation for six years--covering three complete renewal
cycles. This extended retention period protects you during potential audits, which typically review
5-10% of renewals annually. Proper documentation includes:
• **Certificates of completion** showing provider name, course title, completion date, and PDH
hours earned
• **Conference programs** highlighting technical sessions attended
• **Self-study materials** with passing test scores
• **Teaching records** with course syllabi and attendance documentation
Digital record-keeping works perfectly, but organization is crucial. Create a folder structure by re-
newal period, then subdivide by activity type. Name files descriptively: "2024-03-15_ASCE_Struc-
tural_Design_Workshop_4PDH.pdf" beats "Certificate1.pdf" every time. For online courses, screen-
shot confirmation pages immediately after completion--some providers limit certificate access.

FAQs
Q: How many PDH hours does Wisconsin require for PE renewal?
A: Wisconsin requires 30 PDH hours per two-year renewal cycle, including 2 hours of professional
or business ethics. These hours must be completed before your license renewal date to maintain
active status.
Q: What is the CEU to PDH conversion in Wisconsin?
A: 1 CEU equals 10 PDH hours. Multiply any CEU value by 10 to determine the PDH equivalent.
For example, a 0.4 CEU course provides 4 PDH hours.
Q: Can all Wisconsin PE PDH hours come from online courses?
A: Yes. Wisconsin does not cap online or self-study hours--all 30 PDHs may be completed online,
provided courses meet content standards and relate to engineering practice.
