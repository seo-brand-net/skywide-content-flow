Brief explanation of each:

Karst sinkholes: Florida's bedrock is limestone. Groundwater dissolves limestone over thousands of years, creating underground voids. When the soil above can't bridge the void anymore, it collapses. This is the type that swallowed Jeff Bush in Seffner in 2013 and the type the Florida DEP, FGS, and water management districts primarily document.

Waterfront subsidence sinkholes: When a seawall develops cracks, gaps, or seam failures, every tide cycle pulls soil through the wall and into the water. Over months or years, voids form behind the wall. Eventually, the surface gives way: a paver shifts, a depression opens up, or a pit forms in the yard. Homeowners often think they have a karst sinkhole. They usually have a seawall failure.

FAQs
Keep answers 2 to 3 sentences. 5 to 6 FAQs total, mapped to PAA queries.

Are sinkholes a problem in Florida? Yes. Florida has the highest concentration of sinkholes in the United States due to its limestone bedrock geology. Most are concentrated in Central Florida's "Sinkhole Alley" (Pasco, Hernando, and Hillsborough Counties), though sinkholes can occur statewide.
Where is Florida's Sinkhole Alley? Sinkhole Alley refers to a region of Central Florida with the highest density of documented karst sinkholes, primarily Pasco, Hernando, and Hillsborough Counties. Other karst-prone areas include Marion, Citrus, Lake, and Sumter Counties.
What causes sinkholes in Florida? Most Florida sinkholes form when groundwater dissolves the limestone bedrock beneath the surface, creating underground voids that eventually collapse. Drought, heavy rain, and human activity (groundwater pumping, construction) can accelerate the process.
What's the difference between a karst sinkhole and a waterfront sinkhole? Karst sinkholes form from limestone bedrock dissolution and require geotechnical evaluation. Waterfront sinkholes near a seawall typically form when soil migrates through cracks or seams in the wall, leaving voids that collapse, and are addressed by repairing the seawall.
Are sinkholes near my seawall covered by insurance? Usually no, if the sinkhole is caused by seawall failure rather than karst collapse. Florida sinkhole insurance typically applies to geological sinkhole events that meet specific structural criteria. Waterfront subsidence is usually treated as a seawall maintenance issue. Confirm with your insurance carrier.
How do I fix a sinkhole behind my seawall? Repair starts with a professional evaluation to identify the cause. If the cause is seawall failure, fixing the underlying seawall (tieback reinforcement, seam sealing, void filling) stops the soil migration that's creating the sinkhole. Surface restoration follows.
