This process, called karstification, has been shaping Florida's underground landscape for millions of years.\n\nNatural factors accelerate sinkhole formation in ways that groundwater monitoring data clearly demonstrates. During droughts, falling water tables remove the buoyant support that groundwater provides to overlying soils. Florida's aquifer system provides drinking water to millions and serves as the highway for this ongoing dissolution process.\n\nHuman activities significantly impact sinkhole development. Data from municipal water departments shows that even something as simple as a broken water main can accelerate localized dissolution.\n\nBehind seawalls, the causes shift from geological to structural.
