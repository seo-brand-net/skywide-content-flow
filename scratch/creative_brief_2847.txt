(~125 words) Brief, plain-language definition. RAD develops in early childhood (typically before age 5) when a child doesn't form healthy attachment to a primary caregiver, usually due to neglect, abuse, or caregiver disruption. The DSM-5 lists it as a trauma- and stressor-related disorder. Note that disinhibited social engagement disorder (DSED) is a related but separate diagnosis covering \"overly familiar with strangers\" patterns. Both can persist into adulthood. Symptoms typically show across multiple life domains, not just one relationship.\nQ: How do I know if I have reactive attachment disorder as a young adult? Trauma-informed therapy, consistent therapeutic relationships, and sometimes higher levels of care can produce meaningful changes in relational functioning, emotional regulation, and life stability. Progress is gradual.\nQ: Is there a reliable test for RAD in young adults? A: No standardized self-test exists.
