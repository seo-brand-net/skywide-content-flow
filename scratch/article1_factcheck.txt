## Common Seawall Problems in St. Petersburg

Here are the common seawall problems frequently encountered by St. Petersburg homeowners:

- **Cracking**: Wider cracks that signal a stressed wall can lead to structure compromise. Visible long cracks should not be ignored as they worsen over time, increasing repair costs. It's important for homeowners to spot these cracks early and contact professionals to assess the severity.

- **Soil Erosion/Sinkholes**: Erosion often occurs due to soil washing out through seawall joints or cracks. Homeowners might notice depressions or sinkholes forming near the seawall as the first signs. If you see these signs, avoid the affected area and wait for professional evaluation.

- **Tieback Failure/Leaning**: Indicates problems with the anchors that hold the wall in place or issues with water pressure behind the wall. If the wall appears to lean, it's a visible cue of tiebacks failing, risking complete collapse. Barricade access to lean areas to prevent accidents before repairs begin.

- **Hurricane Damage**: Post-storm changes can cause structural weaknesses. Stains or newly formed cracks may appear after such events, signaling immediate need for repairs to prevent future damage. Stay clear of damaged areas and contact repair services promptly to assess hurricane-induced issues.

Local influences such as tides, boat wake, saltwater intrusion, aging concrete seawalls, and issues particular to Pinellas County canals contribute significantly to these problems. Addressing these issues promptly prevents escalating damage and higher repair costs.
