## TONE PROTECTION WARNINGS

### Avoid These Pitfalls:
1. **Do not force keywords into the CTA sentences**. The current "If you are in Berks County and still on the fence, consider having Merkel's Tree Service walk the property with you" reads naturally. Adding keywords here would sound promotional and forced.

2. **Do not add keyword variations to bullet points**. The existing bullet lists (wood chip uses, grinding benefits, removal situations) are clean and scannable. Inserting keywords would disrupt the professional, informational flow.

3. **Preserve the conversational "you" voice**. Phrases like "If you are staring at an old tree stump" and "you are not alone" establish trust. Do not replace these with keyword-heavy alternatives.

4. **Maintain the balanced comparison tone**. The article currently presents both options fairly without pushing one service over another. This informational neutrality aligns with user intent and should not be compromised for keyword density.
